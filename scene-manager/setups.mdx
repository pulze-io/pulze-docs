Setups are the foundation of Scene Manager. Every module - [cameras](/scene-manager/camera-and-framing), [lighting](/scene-manager/lighting), [render settings](/scene-manager/rendering), [scene visibility](/scene-manager/scene-control) - stores its values per setup. Before configuring anything else, you need at least one setup.

## Creating and activating setups

To create an empty setup, press the **+** button at the top right corner. Once you click on a setup it becomes activated, and Scene Manager applies all of its stored settings to your scene.

<Frame caption="Setup List with multiple setups showing camera, resolution, and environment columns">
  <img src="/images/scene-manager/sm_module_setup_list.png" alt="Setup List with multiple setups showing camera, resolution, and environment columns" />
</Frame>

Scene Manager stores all its settings in the current .max file. Whenever you modify or create a setup it will be stored in the scene. This way if you open the max file on another workstation all the setups will be accessible through the Scene Manager.

You can show or hide columns in the setup list from the [Settings](/scene-manager/settings) page.

## Scene Name

After a certain complexity PhysCamera001, PhysCamera002 doesn't mean a lot - with the Scene Name module you can add a more defined name for your scene setup.
